Wild Ghana is a conservation movement that aims at preserving and conserving biodiversity through wildlife photography and videography, blogging, providing training for young conservationists, establishing biodiversity conservation groups in basic and senior high schools, and through social media campaigns.

DONATE
MISSIONS
Wild Ghana’s vision is to shaping minds towards preservation of the wild through audio-visuals communication.

VISIONS
Our mission is to create awareness of various endangered species through a conscious creation of pictorial and visual images of species across the globe.
OBJECTIVES
  1. Improve knowledge on biodiversity in Ghana and across West Africa through wildlife photography, video documentary, animation and blogging.
  2. Instigate environmentally friendly behaviours by showcasing nature and audio-visuals and effective storytelling.
  3. Empower people to become agents of change in biodiversity conservation.
  4. Amplify and scale-up the impact of Herp Conservation Ghana and other conservation bodies to the public.

MEET OUR CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER

Michael Akrasi


Mike is a passionate young conservationist, is the Senior Conservation Officer of Herp-Ghana. He holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Natural Resources Management having specialized in Wildlife and Range Management. He developed predictive modelling maps for threatened and endemic amphibian species in Ghana as his undergraduate thesis. Michael has over two years’ experience in field research work and community awareness campaign. Michael has a passion for wildlife photography and is also interested in learning GIS and Remote Sensing.
